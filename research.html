Research Areas

Decentralized Autonomy

Nousaic investigates architectures for autonomous systems that operate reliably in the absence of centralized control. Our research explores local decision-making under partial observability, agent coordination through emergent structure, and adaptive behavior in degraded environments.

Role Alignment and Coordination Protocols

We develop formal models for dynamic role assignment in multi-agent systems. Our protocols are designed to be robust to message loss, capable of convergence without shared state, and grounded in provable behavioral guarantees under real-world constraints.

Semantically-Grounded Interaction

Effective coordination requires more than message passing. We explore how semantic structure can serve as a shared interface for agents to interpret updates, maintain coherence, and act meaningfully in decentralized contexts.

Systems Under Constraints

Our work is driven by the realities of constrained autonomy: lossy communication, asynchronous execution, incomplete knowledge, and unpredictable environments. We aim to build systems that are not just intelligent, but resilient by design.
